LICENSING. Eat Well Kitchen. 12 Atlantic Avenue. Common Victualler License.
Fernanda Oliveria appeared before the Board seeking a Common Vicutaller License.
Motion made and seconded to approve the application for a Common Victualler License
for Eat Well Kitchen, 12 Atlantic Avenue, hours of operation Monday through Sunday
8:00 a.m. – 4:00 p.m. subject to receipt of all applicable inspection sign offs and all fees
paid. Seating capacity = 16. All in favor.

LICENSING. One Day Entertainment. The Beacon. 123 Pleasant Street. Motion
made and seconded to approve the request from Michael Tumulty, Event Coordinator,
The Beacon Restaurant, 123 Pleasant Street, for a 1 Day Entertainment License for
Thursday, June 30, 2022 from 12:00 noon to 4:00 p.m. All in favor.

CONTRACT. Police Station Exterior Painting. Motion made and seconded to amend
the contract for exterior painting of police station between the Town and John Skouras
and Co Inc. of Peabody, MA by increasing the contract amount four thousand dollars
($4,000.00) and authorize the Chair to sign on behalf of the Board. All in favor.

CONTRACT. State Street Restroom Project. Motion made and seconded to amend
the contract for the state street restroom project between the Town and MJS Construction
of Peabody, MA by increasing the contract amount two thousand nine hundred and
ninety-three and 18/00 dollars (($2,993.18) and authorize the Chair to sign on behalf of
the Board. All in favor.

STATE STREET LANDING. Hoya Lift. Mrs. Belf-Becker noted a dedication
ceremony was held over the weekend at the State Street Landing celebrating the
installment of a Hoya Lift. The lift is the first of its kind on the Harbor. The lift will
permit residents with mobility limitations to safely board boats. Purchase of the Hoya lift
was made possible through a private donation and in coordination with the Disabilities
Commission and the Harbor Masters office.

GRANT. The Chair advised the Board that Marblehead has been awarded a $100,000
MassTrails Grant for the Marblehead-Salem Regional Gateway Trail Connection.
Rebecca Cutting, Town Planner, advised to the Board that this grant would be for the
design and permitting, with the design being consistent with the Town’s Rail Trail Master
Plan. The Board thanked Ms. Cutting for her work on this.

Motion made and seconded to adjourn at 10:35 a.m. All in favor.
