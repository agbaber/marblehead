Technology Modernization and Customer Service Improvements – OpenGov
Platform
As part of the Town’s ongoing technology modernization and customer service
improvement efforts, the Procurement Office has fully transitioned to a paperless, online
bidding process using the OpenGov platform. This system modernizes how the Town
conducts procurement by providing vendors with a single login, mobile-friendly access,
and real-time notifications, making it easier for businesses of all sizes to identify and
respond to bid opportunities. For residents and vendors alike, the platform improves
transparency by offering clear, timely access to procurement information and bid status
updates.

The adoption of OpenGov also supports more efficient internal operations by reducing
manual paperwork, shortening processing times, and improving compliance with public
procurement requirements. These efficiencies allow staff to focus more time on service
delivery and oversight while ensuring procurement processes remain fair, competitive,
and accountable.

Village Street Bridge (Harold B. Breare Bridge) – TIP Application Submission
The Harold B Breare Bridge project, commonly known as the Village Street Bridge, has
been officially submitted to the Boston Region Metropolitan Planning Organization
(MPO) for inclusion in the FFY 2027–2031 Transportation Improvement Program (TIP).
The application seeks construction funding based on an estimated construction cost of
$5.25 million, which includes a 10 percent contingency. Staff from multiple departments
contributed technical input, coordination, review, and supporting documentation to
advance the funding submission.

The project scope includes full replacement of the Village Street Bridge, associated
roadway and sidewalk improvements, and construction of an ADA-compliant pedestrian
access path connecting Village Street to the Rail Trail below. The project is currently at
the 25 percent design stage, which is required for TIP eligibility, and is undergoing
technical review by MassDOT. The project team will continue to monitor
communications from the MPO and provide updates as the project advances through both
the TIP programming process and subsequent design milestones.

Picket House – Exterior Improvements
Exterior improvements at the Picket House continue to progress. Siding and trim
replacement has been completed on three sides of the building. A new composite gutter
has been ordered and will be installed upon arrival. In addition, all windows have been
measured, and the Town is awaiting a quotation from Harvey Windows. Once pricing is
received, window orders will be placed.

Franklin Street Fire Station – Building Envelope and Mechanical Upgrades
Work at the Franklin Street Fire Station has included the completion of siding
replacement on two sides of the building, as well as the replacement of rotted trim along
the front façade. Measurements have been taken for all remaining windows, and a
quotation has been received. Window orders are scheduled to be placed this week.
In addition to exterior improvements, a new heating system boiler has been ordered to
replace the existing 30-year-old unit. As part of this upgrade, a SuperStor hot water
storage tank will be installed in conjunction with the new boiler. All components are
expected to arrive this week, and installation work will begin shortly thereafter.

PUBLIC SHADE TREE HEARING. 2 Thomas Circle. The Chair opened the public
hearing on the application from Wren Sooy and Kristin Horgan, 2 Thomas Circle, to
remove a public shade tree. Wren Sooy presented her application to the Board. Jon
Fobert, Tree Warden, Certified Arborist, addressed the Board stating he felt the tree in
question was in good health and that this may be more a sidewalk issue with the tree
roots and noted that should the Board approve this application the cost to the applicant
would be approximately $6,000, or planting 12 trees, in accordance with the Town of
Marblehead’s Shade Tree Policy. The Board received 2 letters on file opposed to the
application. Bob Sooy spoke in favor of the application. The Chair closed the public
hearing. The Board reviewed the application and stated support for Tree Warden and the
Town’s Shade Tree Policy. After discussion a motion was made and seconded to deny
the application from Wren Sooy and Kristin Horgan for the removal of a public shade
tree at 2 Thomas Circle. All in favor.

PUBLIC SHADE TREE HEARING. 7 Widger Road. The Chair opened the public
hearing on the application from Stephen Cummings, Building Commissioner, to remove
three public shade trees located at 7 Widger Road. The Building Commissioner
presented the application to the Board. There are two main concerns for requesting
removal of the trees: 1. The Town’s Fiber Loop (main hub) goes through the canopy of
one of the trees and 2. damage that will occur to the new roof due to the proximity of the
trees to the building and the amount of pinecones that fall onto the roof which could
puncture the new material as well as the sap from the pinecones causing damage. Jon
Fobert, Tree Warden, Certified Arborist, addressed the Board and reviewed work that has
been done on the trees and potential work that could be done to mitigate some of the
concerns. Wren Sooy spoke opposed to this application. The Board received two letters
in opposition to the application and placed them on file. Al Jordon spoke in favor of the
application. The Chair closed the public hearing.

The Board had a lengthy discussion with the Building Commissioner and Tree Warden
reviewing possible options including a pruning plan that could reduce damage to the
Fiber Loop and roof as well as implementing more advantageous maintenance of the
roofs. The Board felt it was a large request to remove three public shade trees and asked
the Building Commissioner and Tree Warden to work together to try and find other
solutions. The Board will continue the public hearing. Motion made and seconded to
continue the public shade tree hearing until March 25, 2026. All in favor.
