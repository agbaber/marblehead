VETERANS AGENT. Memorial Day. David Rodgers, Veterans’ Agent, appeared
before the Board to announce the following Memorial Day events:
Friday, May 24, 2019. 9:00 a.m. Veterans Breakfast at the Council on Aging
sponsored by the Masons.
Saturday, May 25, 2019. 9:00 a.m. from the Bell Tower at Waterside Cemetery.
Veteran’s Grave Flagging. All are welcome to participate in honoring Marblehead’s
Veterans. Anyone needing a flag for a Veteran’s Gravemay call the Veterans Office at
781-631-0990.
Sunday, May 26, 2019. 1:00 p.m. Veterans Service at Star of the Sea Cemetery.
Public is welcome.
Monday, May 27, 2019. Memorial Day Parade. 8:00 a.m. Muster at Old Town
House. 9:00 a.m. main parade begins; march to Memorial Park then to Waterside
Cemetery. Honorary Grand Marshal Richard Bridgeo, US Coast Guard/US Merchant
Marine WWII, Principle speaker, Ben Gatchell, USMC. Trolley service will be available
from the VFW at 7:30 a.m. for all Veterans who may need assistance to participate in the
activities. Breakfast will be served at the VFW after the ceremonies. The public is
invited and encouraged to attend all ceremonies.

HISTORICAL COMMISSION. Mapping Marblehead Exhibit. Old Town House.
Chris Johnston Chairman, Marblehead Historical Commission and Pam Peterson,
member, appeared before the Board to announce Part 1 of the Museum of Marblehead
History: Mapping Marblehead: from Founding to Freedom (Pre-history to 1800). The
exhibit includes maps, displays, descriptive panels and technology and a photo
opportunity with George Washington and Glover’s men. The exhibit will take place at
the Old Town House, June 1 – 23, 2019: Thursdays 1:00 p.m. – 7:00 p.m. with a
reception from 5:00pm – 7:00 p.m. and Fridays, Saturdays and Sundays from 1:00 p.m. –
4:00 p.m. Docents will be present to answer any questions. Admission is free.

MARBLEHEAD FOREVER COMMITTEE. Request to donate. Motion made and
seconded to approve the request from the Marblehead Forever Committee to transfer
$1600.00 from the Town Celebration Trust Fund to the Historical Commission to assist
in the design of a brochure for the exhibit “Mapping Marblehead: from Founding to
Freedom”. All in favor.

PUBLIC HEARING. Licensing. Change of Officers/Directors, Transfer of Stock,
Change of Manager. Brijes Corporation. 260 Humphrey Street. Rohitkumar K.
Patel, 1029 Broadway, Somerville, MA, appeared before the Board as it relates to an
application from Brijes Corporation, 260 Humphrey Street, to change the Officers and
Directors, Transfer of Stock and Change of Manager on their All Alcoholic Beverage
Package Store License. No one spoke opposed or in favor of the application. Motion
made and seconded to approve the application from Brijes Corporation, d/b/a Beach
Bluff Liquors, 260 Humphrey Street, to change the Officers and Directors, Transfer of
Stock and Change of Manager, Rohitkumar K. Patel, as presented, on the All Alcoholic
Beverage Package Store License, #88860-PK-0656, subject to approval from the ABCC
and receipt of all required forms. On a polled vote the Board voted as follows: Mr.
Grader, in favor; Mr. Christensen, in favor; Mrs. Jacobi, in favor; Mr. Nye, in favor; Mrs.
Belf-Becker, in favor.

LICENSING. Boston Yacht Club. Change of Officers/Directors. Brian Pelland,
General Manager, Boston Yacht Club, 1 Front Street, appeared before the Board as it
relates to an application from the Boston Yacht Club to change the Officers/Directors on
the All Alcoholic Beverage Club License. Motion made and seconded to approve the
application as submitted by the Boston Yacht Club, 1 Front Street, for Change of Officers
and Directors on the All Alcoholic Beverage Club License subject to receipt of the
required forms, fees and approval from the Alcoholic Beverage Control Commission
(ABCC). On a polled vote the Board voted as follows: Mr. Grader, in favor; Mr.
Christensen, in favor; Mr. Nye, in favor; Mrs. Jacobi, in favor; Mrs. Belf-Becker, in
favor.

NORTH SHORE PRIDE. Flag. Hope Watt-Bucci, President, and Sherry Gagne,
Board of Directors, appeared before the Board requesting that the Town raise the Pride
flag in June, during Pride Month, to honor, commemorate and celebrate diversity and the
LGBTQ community. Motion made and seconded to approve the request from Sherry

Gagne and Hope Watt-Bucci, to raise the Pride flag during the month of June; the Town
Administrator will coordinate duration of time the flag shall be on the pole, beneath the
American Flag, and the Town Administrator shall work with Town Counsel to develop a
policy, to bring to the Board, regarding flags, other than the American Flag, to be flown
beneath the American Flag, on Town flagpoles for any requests hereafter. All in favor.
