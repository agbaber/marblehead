FORT SEWALL OVERSIGHT COMMITTEE. Update. June Celebration. Larry
Sands, Chairman, Fort Sewall Oversight Committee, appeared before the Board to update
the Board on the Committee’s work and seek permission for various events and projects
at the Fort. In anticipation of the celebration in June the following items are being
attended to; Landscaping is being completed, a replica cannon will be delivered by the
first of June and Glovers Regiment is seeking permission to fire the cannon on
ceremonial occasions, the donor plaque is being fabricated and will be installed, details
are being finalized for the Ranger program, a Fort Sewall commemorative book has been
designed and will be published and new signs and security cameras are being installed.
The Town recently received a $2,000 grant from the Essex Heritage Partnership to outfit
the inside of the Fort and an Eagle Scout project is proposed to install flag holders on the
new slit rail fence to hold the flairs on the annual July 4th celebration with small
memorial plaques attached commemorating 16 soldiers from the American Revolution.

PAVEMENT MANAGEMENT PROGRAM. Presentation. Conrad Leger,
Environmental Partners and Robert Dever, Public Works Director, appeared before the
Board to present a Pavement Management Program to the Town. Pavement Management
is the practice of planning pavement maintenance to maximize the value of the roadway
network. Infrastructure asset management is done in a 3-step project approach; 1.
network inventory and data collection; 2. Analysis and reporting; 3. Capital planning and
action planning. The Interim Town Administrator advised the Board that they will be
discussing funding for this program later in their meeting when they review the warrant
articles.
