WINTER PARKING BAN. Motion made and seconded that the winter parking ban
will commence at 12:01 a.m., Monday, November 26, 2018, and continue until April 15,
2019. No parking is allowed on any street for a period of time longer than one (1) hour
between the hours of midnight and 7:00 a.m. In the case of a snow emergency prior to
November 26, 2018 all cars will be required to be off the public way and the winter
parking ban would be in effect immediately. This information is posted on the Town’s
website, www.marblehead.org, at the entrances to Town, on MHTV and in all local
newspapers. No Warning Tickets will be given. Do not park your car on the street
overnight on Sunday night. All in favor.

SHELLFISHING. Conditionally Approved. Motion made and seconded, in
accordance with Chapter 130, section 74A of Massachusetts General Laws, the Division
of Marine Fisheries has determined that the shellfish classification area, N21.2 Devereux
and Tuckers Beaches, classified as CONDITIONALLY APPROVED, now meets the established
criteria for the harvest of shellfish. As a result, the status of this area has been changed to
“OPEN TO SHELLFISHING” October 1, 2018 – April 30, 2019. The status of the above
conditional area, N21.2, shall automatically change to “CLOSED TO SHELLFISHING”
on May 1, 2019, unless the Division of marine Fisheries changes the area’s status prior to
said date. All in favor.

TOWN CLERK. Elections. Absentee Voting. Early Voting. The last day to register
to vote for the November 6, 2018 General Election is Wednesday, October 17, 2018.
Any person who is currently registered to vote does not have to re-register. Only
individuals who are registering to vote for the first time, moved into town, or within the
Town of Marblehead, or want to change their party affiliation need to register before
October 19, 2018. The Town Clerk’s office will be open on Wednesday, October 17,
2018 until 8 p.m. for voter registration. To check to see if you are registered to vote, and
to find information on how to register to vote, you may visit the Secretary of the
Commonwealth’s website: www.sec.state.ma.us/ele. You can register to vote, change

your address, or party affiliation, online if you have a Massachusetts Driver’s License or
Massachusetts issued ID by visiting https://www.sec.state.ma.us/OVR/
ABSENTEE BALLOTS are available. Voters are able to vote absentee if you are absent
from the town on Election Day, a physical disability preventing you from going to the
polling location, or a religious belief. You can apply for an absentee ballot until 12:00PM
on November 5, 2018.
