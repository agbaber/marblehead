TOWN ADMINISTRATOR UPDATE.
The Town Administrator provided a consolidated summary of key ongoing projects,
operational updates, and upcoming initiatives currently underway across town
departments as follows:

Open Finance Transparency Center – MUNIS
The Finance Department has completed implementation of the new Open Finance
Transparency Center within our MUNIS financial system. This new platform enhances
public transparency by providing direct access to current fiscal data.

The site currently displays budgeted revenues, budgeted expenditures, actual
expenditures, and vendor payments. Because the Town’s prior revenue data remains in
our legacy financial system, historical revenue figures have not yet been imported and
therefore are not reflected in the current view. That work will continue as part of our
broader ERP transition process.

Unlike ClearGov, which is updated periodically, the Open Finance portal updates daily,
providing residents with near real-time financial information. Certain sensitive data fields
have been appropriately removed to prevent potential fraud or misuse.

Importantly, the Town will continue to maintain ClearGov for historical financial data
from our legacy system, ensuring continuity and long-term transparency.

In coordination with the Finance Department the Town will make a public announcement
and place all this information on the Town website.

Transition to New Mass Notification System – Regroup
As previously reported, the Town’s current mass notification platform, CodeRED (now
known as Crises24), experienced a cyber-attack that affected Marblehead and numerous
other communities, significantly impacting the reliability of the system. Throughout this
winter season, the Town deployed the system multiple times during weather events and
encountered several complications, including inconsistent delivery to subscribers, unclear
recorded messages, and calls appearing to originate from out-of-state numbers. These
issues understandably affected public confidence in our emergency communication
capabilities.

Given that we remain in the middle of the winter season and rely heavily on timely
emergency notifications, we moved quickly to evaluate alternative platforms. After
review, we have decided to migrate to the Regroup mass notification system. Regroup
will provide reliable, rapid emergency messaging capabilities, as well as enhanced
outreach tools that will allow us to broaden and improve our communication with
residents beyond emergency notifications.

The cost of a one-year agreement is $6,500. Although an item was placed on tonight’s
Select Board agenda in anticipation of potential contract approval requirements, prior to
final confirmation of pricing, the final cost falls below the $10,000 approval threshold
and is also supported by an existing budgeted line item. Accordingly, Select Board
approval is not required.

This agreement includes full migration of our existing contact data by the vendor. Chief
Gilliland and members of the public safety team will work directly with Regroup to
ensure the system is transitioned and operational as quickly as possible.

Given the recent difficulties with our current platform, I felt it was important to
communicate this action publicly to assure residents that we have acted swiftly to secure
a reliable mass notification system that meets Marblehead’s needs during the winter
season and beyond.

FY27 BUDGET TIMELINE DISCUSSION. The Town Administrator provided a memo,
and reviewed with the Select Board, the FY207 Budget Development Timeline and status
update. A lengthy discussion followed focusing on the process of creating a balanced
budget for the town, prioritizing departments and making tough funding decisions. Select
Board members emphasized the need for a multi-year outlook, including staffing,
employee costs, and capital needs, as well as data on long-term consequences of budget
cuts. They also suggested isolating the costs of individual services and exploring
outsourcing options. The objective is to build a balanced budget within the next two
weeks, with additional analysis to follow in the process.

Town Administrator and Chief Financial Officer will present a balanced budget proposal
for FY27, as well as potential override options, at the next Select Board meeting on
February 25, 2026. The Board asked the Town Administrator to develop multi-year
budget projections (3-year outlook) showing both a "restored service" scenario and a
"smart invest" scenario and do an analysis by department, staffing/employee changes,
capital requests, and potential long-term consequences of proposed cuts. The Board
discussed various means to get all this information out to the public, suggesting
scheduling "Municipal Boot Camp" sessions in March for residents to learn about town
departments.

SNOW EMERGENCY DECLARATION. Authority to Delegate. In an effort to
coordinate more efficiently when a Snow Emergency Declaration is needed the Town
Administrator is seeking the Board’s approval to delegate this duty to him. A brief
discussion was held on snow removal and a motion was made and seconded to delegate
to the Town Administrator, in coordination with the Director of Public Works, the Police
Chief, and the Fire Chief, the authority to declare a Snow Emergency on an as-needed
basis when significant snow accumulation from forecasted or actual weather conditions
poses an immediate threat to the health or safety of persons or property in the Town. The
Town Administrator, Director of Public Works, Police Chief, and Fire Chief shall
establish the start and end times of any declared Snow Emergency as necessary to
maintain and protect public ways, emergency access, and pedestrian safety. Any
declaration made pursuant to this motion shall include notification to the Conservation
Commission where applicable, required reporting to the Massachusetts Department of
Environmental Protection (MassDEP), and notice to the Select Board at the earliest
practicable time. This delegation shall automatically terminate at the conclusion of the
2025–2026 winter season, unless earlier amended or rescinded by vote of the Select
Board. All in favor.
