HEARING. Dangerous Dog. The Town Administrator advised the Board that a public
hearing on a dangerous dog(s) was held on Monday, November 19, 2018 at 4:00 pm, in
accordance with Mass General Laws, Chapter 140, section 157, to determine if the dog
residing at 59 Humphrey Street is a nuisance dog or dangerous dog. Betsy Cruger,
Animal Control Officer, was present at the hearing. Testimony was heard from the

Animal Control Officer and from Mark Lausier and Gene Arnold. Letters of support
from Joanne McElhenney, Veronica Hobson and Brian Stephens were submitted by
Timothy Bradl, Counsel for the dog owners and read into the record. Based upon the
evidence presented and the recommendations of the Animal Control Officer, the Town
Administrator recommends that the Board of Selectmen approve the following conditions
to be imposed on Sassy, the dog residing at 59 Humphrey Street:
    1. When removed from the premises, Sassy be securely and humanely muzzled and
        restrained with a leash that meets Mass General Laws criteria “chain or tethering
        device with a minimum tensile strength of 300 lbs., not to exceed 3 feet in
        length”;
    2. The owner of Sassy shall provide proof of insurance in accordance with Mass
        General Law, in an amount of no less than $100,000, insuring the owner against
        any claim, loss, damage or injury to persons, pets or property resulting from the
        acts, whether intentional or unintentional of the dog, or that proof of reasonable
        efforts to obtain such insurance were made, if a policy has not been issued;
    3. The owner provide information by which Sassy can be identified for the rest of
        her life, including but not limited to photos, a microchip and a vet examination
    4. Sassy is NOT to be walked with any other dogs.
Motion made and seconded to approve the recommendations as presented and to notify
the dog’s owner in writing. All in favor.

CHRISTMAS TREE BURNING / REMOVAL. Christmas Trees will be picked up at
the curbside from Wednesday, December 26, 2018 through Friday, January 4, 2019 and
will be placed at Riverhead Beach for the community bonfire on Sunday, January 6,
2019. The Christmas tree pile will be set alight at 6:00 p.m. Residents are invited to
attend this community event on January 6, 2019. Christmas Trees will continue to be
picked up curbside after January 4, 2019 until January 11, 2019. After January 11, 2019
residents should bring trees to the town’s transfer station for proper disposal. All trees
left curbside for pick up from December 26, 2018 until January 11, 2019 must have all
lights, ornaments, and stands removed and not be in plastic bags. Any tree not meeting
these requirements will not be picked up. In addition, holiday wreaths, roping and
garland will not be picked up.
