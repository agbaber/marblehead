                             BOARD OF SELECTMEN

                                     MINUTES

                                    April 10, 2019

Board met in session at 7:30 p.m. in the Selectmen’s Meeting Room at Abbot Hall
Present (constituting a quorum):

                                Judith R. Jacobi, Chair
                                Harry C. Christensen Jr
                                 M. C. Moses Grader
                                     James E. Nye

                           Jason Silva, Town Administrator

CHAIRMAN PRO TEMP. Motion made and seconded to appoint Judith R. Jacobi
Chairman Pro Temp for this meeting. All in favor.

PUBLIC HEARING. Licensing. Transfer/Pledge. All Alcoholic Package Store
License. Woodfin Spirits, Inc. Attorney Paul Lynch and Frank Bertolino, appeared
before the Board seeking to Transfer and Pledge the All Alcoholic Package Store License
at 1Woodfin Terrace. No one spoke in favor or opposed to the applicant. Motion made
and seconded to approve the application to Transfer and Pledge the All Alcoholic
Package Store License from Beacon Hill Import Co., Inc. to Woodfin Spirits Inc., d/b/a
Beacon Hill Imports, 1 Woodfin Terrace, Manager Frank Bertolino, 54 New Castle Road,
Peabody, hours of operation Monday through Saturday 8:00am – 11:00 p.m., Sundays
10:00 a.m. - 11:00 p.m. subject to approval from the ABCC, receipt of all applicable
inspection sign offs ,all fees paid and CORI approval. On a polled vote the Board voted
as follows: Mr. Grader, in favor; Mr. Christensen, in favor; Mr. Nye, in favor; Mrs.
Jacobi, in favor.

LICENSING. Eastern Yacht Club. Change of Officers/Directors. Jeff Wargo,
General Manager, 47 Foster Street, appeared before the Board as it relates to an
application from the Eastern Yacht Club to change the Officers/Directors on the All
Alcoholic Beverage Club License. Motion made and seconded to approve the application
as submitted by the Eastern Yacht Club, 47 Foster Street, for Change of Officer and
Directors on the All Alcoholic Beverage Club License subject to receipt of the required
forms, fees and approval from the Alcoholic Beverage Control Commission (ABCC).
On a polled vote the Board voted as follows: Mr. Grader, in favor; Mr. Christensen, in
favor; Mr. Nye, in favor; Mrs. Jacobi, in favor.

ANIMAL INSPECTOR. Re-appointment. Motion made and seconded to reappoint
Daniel Proulx as Animal Inspectors for the Town of Marblehead effective May 1, 2019.
All in favor.

CONTRACT. Bituminous Concrete. Extend. Motion made and seconded to extend
the contract between the Town and Aggregate Industries Northeast Region of Saugus,
MA, Bituminous Concrete Pick Up, for an additional second year option to begin July 1,
2019 and to authorize the Chari to sign the amendment of behalf of the Board. All in
favor.

PERMISSION. Abbot Hall. Bell Ringing. National Prayer Day. Motion made and
seconded to approve request from Donald Orne to ring Abbot Hall’s bell at noon on
Thursday, May 2, 2019 for five minutes directly following the noon regular ringing, for
National Prayer Day, and to meet for 10 minutes in the Selectmen’s Meeting Room to
pray and meditate as one unified group. This event is open to the public.

FORT SEWALL. Permission to Use. Glover’s Regiment. Motion made and
seconded to approve the request from Seamus Daly, Captain, Glover’s Marblehead
Regiment, to use Fort Sewall for the Regiment’s annual summer encampment Friday,
July 12, 2019 to Sunday, July 14, 2019 subject to the usual rules, regulations and receipt
of the required Certificate of Insurance. All in favor.
