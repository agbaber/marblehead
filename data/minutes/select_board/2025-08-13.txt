Cove Lane Property Ownership
The Town, in coordination with its legal counsel, is actively reviewing historical property
records and deeds to determine the precise ownership status of Cove Lane, a roadway
that provides access to a beach area. For decades, it had been generally understood that
the Town held full ownership of the lane based on transactions dating back to the early
1900s. However, preliminary research now indicates that Cove Lane may be jointly
owned by the Town and several private property owners whose parcels abut the roadway.
The beach area at the end of the lane is confirmed to be Town-owned and remains open
to the public without dispute.

Recently, some private residents have asserted that they hold sole ownership of Cove
Lane and have suggested the possibility of restricting public access. Under Massachusetts
property law, if the lane is jointly owned, no single owner has the authority to block or
restrict the rights of access of any other owner, including the Town. This joint ownership
status would therefore preserve the Town’s legal right to use and maintain the property
for public benefit.

The Town’s legal counsel is now conducting a detailed title examination and historical
deed review to confirm the official ownership record. Once this research is complete, the

Cliff Street Boatyard Cleanup
The cleanup of the Cliff Street Boatyard is now in its final stage and represents a
successful conclusion to a long-standing environmental compliance matter with the
Massachusetts Department of Environmental Protection (MassDEP). Vertex has prepared
a draft Permanent Solution with No Conditions (PSNC) Statement for the Underground
Storage Tank (UST) Release closure. This statement is the highest level of closure
determination available under the Massachusetts Contingency Plan (MCP) for
environmental cleanups. A PSNC means that all required environmental remediation
work has been completed to a standard that ensures no significant risk to human health,
safety, public welfare, or the environment, both now and in the foreseeable future. The
site can be used without any restrictions, special monitoring, or ongoing controls. This
outcome not only resolves a regulatory obligation but also restores full, unrestricted use
of the site, eliminating potential liability and ensuring environmental safety for the
community.

Smith Street Rail Trail Crossing Ribbon-Cutting
On Tuesday, August 26, 2025, at 4:00 PM, the Town will hold a ribbon-cutting at the
Rail Trail crossing at Smith Street (near the Post Office) to celebrate completion of the
Marblehead Rail Trail Showcase Project and to share information on the Marblehead Rail
Trail Master Plan. The Showcase Project delivers visible, near-term upgrades that
demonstrate the Town’s long-range vision for the corridor—improving safety and
accessibility, enhancing the user experience, and introducing consistent wayfinding and
amenities—while inviting public input on the full master plan. A brief program of
remarks and an overview of next steps will follow the ribbon-cutting. The event is open
to the public and all are encouraged to attend.

POLICIES. Green Marblehead Communities Program. Zero Emissions Vehicle
Policy. Logan Casey, Sustainability Coordinator, appeared before the Board to present
an overview of the Commonwealth’s Green Communities Program and outline
Marblehead’s pathway to designation. The Town’s application is due December 2025.
The presentation covered:
    • Program Overview: Green Communities provides grant funding and technical
       assistance for municipal energy efficiency and decarbonization. Nearly 300
       Massachusetts communities have joined, with initial awards of $150,000–
       $170,000 and access to competitive grants up to $225,000 per round.
    • Marblehead’s Progress: The 2018 Town Meeting vote for 100% carbon-free
       energy and the 2023 Net Zero Roadmap (adopted by the Board) set clear climate
       goals of Net Zero Greenhouse Gas Emissions by 2040. Designation as a Green
       Community is a strategic priority in the Net Zero Roadmap.
    • Application Requirements: there are five required criteria. Focus was made on
       two key items for Board action:
               1. Adoption of a Zero Emissions Vehicle (ZEV) First Policy, and
               2. Development and adoption of a Municipal Energy Reduction Plan.
    • ZEV First Policy: This draft policy prioritizes electric vehicles in future fleet
       purchases starting in FY2027, with appropriate exemptions and fuel-efficiency
       standards.

HOST COMMUNITY AGREEMENT. Seven Leaf Sisters, Inc., 385 Atlantic
Avenue. The Board reviewed a memo from the Town Administrator regarding the Host
Community Agreement between the Town and Seven Leaf Sisters, Inc., 385 Atlantic
Avenue. Marblehead currently has two Host Community Agreements in place for adult
retail cannabis sales, one of which is Seven Leaf Sisters, Inc. Licensing is done through
the Cannabis Control Commission at the state level with an entire vetting process. At the
local level it is a requirement of these entities to enter into a host community agreement
with the local municipality, which sets up such terms. The existing agreement, in place
with Seven Leaf Sisters, Inc., has a transaction scheduled with the Cannabis Control
Commission for KCCS LLC, to buy out or purchase the Seven Leaf Sisters, Inc.
Under the current Host Community Agreement with the Town, the Select Board are
required to vote to authorize the assignment of the host community agreement from
Seven Leaf Sisters Inc., to KCCS, LLC, and to maintain all the terms currently in place.
This vote would be contingent upon approval by the Cannabis Control Commission.
After discussion the following motion was made:

Motion made and seconded to approve the assignment of the Host Community
Agreement for Seven Leaf Sisters, Inc. to KCCS, LLC, and amendment agreement,
contingent upon approval by the Cannabis Control Commission. All in favor.
