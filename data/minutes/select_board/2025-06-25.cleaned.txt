TOWN ADMINISTRATOR UPDATE. The Town Administrator updated the Board as
follows:
Veteran Services Agent Speaking Engagements
Veteran Services Officer Roseann “Ro” Trionfi-Mazzuchelli reports that activity has
increased significantly, with a noticeable rise in Vietnam-era veterans coming forward to
seek VA disability benefits. Monthly coffee events have proven to be an excellent
outreach tool, with the next scheduled for July. Additional outreach included the Veterans
Agent speaking at the Deborah Sampson event at the library. Deborah
Sampson was a Revolutionary War hero who disguised herself as a man to fight for her
country. Preparations are underway to speak at several nursing homes to ensure accurate
information is shared about VA benefits. Many veteran homeowners have expressed
appreciation for the recent increase in the property tax abatement passed at Town
Meeting.

MUNIS Finance Software Launch Next Week – the official transition to the MUNIS
financial software system is set for July 1. This upgrade represents a significant step
forward in modernizing the Town’s financial infrastructure, offering enhanced
capabilities in accounting, reporting, budgeting, and operational efficiency.

Salary Survey and Classification Study Launch - Marblehead has launched a
comprehensive classification and compensation study aimed at modernizing its employee

classification system and ensuring the Town's salary structure remains competitive with
comparable and neighboring communities. Led by consultant Katie Yee from MGT, the
study will review existing job descriptions, align positions within the organization, and
analyze market data to determine appropriate compensation levels.

Fourth of July Event Preparation – Preparations are in full swing for Marblehead’s 4th
of July celebrations, with Town departments and volunteers coordinating to ensure a safe
and enjoyable experience. Public safety officials, including the Harbormaster and US
Coast Guard, are establishing a safety zone around the barge, which will be patrolled and
marked for boaters’ awareness. The Police Department is finalizing its operational plan
for the day, including crowd and traffic management, and is coordinating closely with
Brown Island trustees and other key stakeholders. The Fire Department is preparing for
warm weather conditions and will have cooling shelters on standby, while Public Works
is completing infrastructure projects and ensuring facilities are ready for the influx of
visitors.

JULY 4th ANNUAL FIREWORKS DISPLAY. The Town Administrator reviewed
correspondence from Alexandar Falk, Marblehead Fireworks Committee relating to
details around the annual Fireworks and Harbor Illumination set for July 4th. Flares will
be lit at 8:45 p.m. and the fireworks show will begin at 9:00 p.m. Residents can check
the Fireworks Committee’s website (marbleheadfire.works) for updates on weather,
traffic, parking, safety advisories, and drone restrictions. In the event of severe weather,
the rain date for the fireworks is July 5th. The fireworks display is 100% privately
funded, and donations are still being accepted via check, online at
https://fireworksforever.org, or through Venmo @marbleheadfireworks.

STORMWATER MANAGEMENT. Annual Review. Maggie Wheeler, Town
Engineer, appeared before the Board to present the annual review of Marblehead's
Stormwater Management Plan, highlighting the town's compliance with the Federal
Clean Water Act as a Phase 2 municipal separate storm sewer system. Maggie discussed
the importance of maintaining clean waterways by preventing pollutants from entering
the stormwater system, emphasizing public education, illicit discharge elimination, and
construction site runoff control. The issue of pet waste pollution was addressed, urging
residents to properly dispose of pet waste to prevent contamination of town waterways,
and mentioned ongoing dye testing and infrastructure improvements to ensure proper
drainage and water quality.

Mrs. Singer joined the meeting.

BOARD OF HEALTH. Creating a Healthier Marblehead. Thomas Massaro, Chair,
Board of Health, and Andrew Petty, Public Health Director, appeared before the Board to
present their program aimed at strengthening public health in Marblehead, Creating a
Healthier Marblehead (CAHM). Tom highlighted the need to move from a reactive to a
proactive approach. He discussed plans to conduct a health survey with UMass Boston,
aiming for a 30-50% response rate from the town's population. The survey results will
help establish a baseline health status and inform future public health initiatives. Tom
emphasized that no town tax money will be used for this project, which is funded through
grants and donations. The Board of Health aims to make public health more accountable
and integrated with clinical medicine, focusing on promoting wellness rather than just
preventing problems.

RECREATION AND PARKS. Larz Anderson Trust. Shelley Bedrosian, Karen
Ernst and Larry Simpon, Recreation and Parks Commission, appeared before the Board
to review their plans on the Lars Anderson proposal for a multi-phase project at Green
Street to create a weather-independent sports facility. Karen presented an overview of the
project, which includes a refrigerated ice rink with a pavilion-style roof, modular turf,
and a community space. The first phase, estimated at $2.6 million, will be funded by the
Lars Anderson bequest and is expected to break ground in May 2026. The project aims to
provide consistent ice time for youth hockey, host other sports, and serve as a community
space without municipal impact. The team plans to issue an RFP in July for design and
construction management, with a goal to complete phase one by November 2026.

VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES. The Town’s website will be updated to reflect the
current list of vacancies on Town Boards, Commissions and Committees.
The Select Board appoints residents to over 30 boards, committees and commission in the
Town. The Board accepts letters of interest regardless of a vacancy and will keep such
letters on file. The Board will notify you if a vacancy occurs during the year. We strongly
encourage anyone interested in applying for a position on a board/committee/commission
to attend a regularly scheduled meeting of that board/committee/commission and to reach
out to the Chair of that board/committee/commission to gain further insight and a better
understanding of the committee’s purview, scope and what the time expectations may be.
Contact information for all boards/committees/commissions can be found on the website
at www.marbleheadma.gov or by contacting the Select Boards office at 781.631.0000 or
email wileyk@marbleheadma.gov. Anyone interested in serving on one of these Boards
or Committees should submit a cover letter and resume to the Select Board, Abbot Hall,
188 Washington Street or email to wileyk@marbleheadma.gov for more information.
