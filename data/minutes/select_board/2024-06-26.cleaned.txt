VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITIES. Vacancies. The Town’s website will be updated
to reflect the current list of vacancies on Town Boards, Commissions and Committees.
The Select Board appoints residents to over 30 boards, committees and commission in the
Town. The Board accepts letters of interest regardless of a vacancy and will keep such
letters on file. The Board will notify you if a vacancy occurs during the year. We strongly
encourage anyone interested in applying for a position on a board/committee/commission
to attend a regularly scheduled meeting of that board/committee/commission and to reach
out to the Chair of that board/committee/commission to gain further insight and a better
understanding of the committee’s purview, scope and what the time expectations may be.
Contact information for all boards/committees/commissions can be found on the website
at www.marblehead.org or by contacting the Select Boards office at 781.631.0000 or
email wileyk@marblehead.org. Anyone interested in serving on one of these Boards or
Committees should submit a cover letter and resume to the Select Board, Abbot Hall, 188
Washington Street or email to wileyk@marblehead.org for more information.

PURPLE HEART DESIGNATION. CORPORAL WILLIAM BERRY
COMMEMORATIVE. Approval to post signs. Teresa Collins appeared before the
Board asking the Town to designate Marblehead as a Purple Heart Town. Established by
George Washington on August 7, 1782, the Purple Heart is the oldest active military
award in the United States. There are an estimated 1.9 million service members who
have been awarded the Purple Heart. The signs have been designed and purchased at no

Corporal William Berry. Teresa Collins has researched the story of Corporal William
