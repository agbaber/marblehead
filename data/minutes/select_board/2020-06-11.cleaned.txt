OUTDOOR SEATING. The following applications were reviewed by the Board for
outdoor seating.
Café Italia. 10 School Street. Donna Oliviero appeared before the Board seeking
permission for outdoor seating. After review, motion made and seconded, pursuant to
Executive Order COVID-19, No. 35, issued by Governor Baker on June 1, 2020, and
notwithstanding the provisions of Chapter 40A of the General Laws, or any special
permit, variance or other approvals thereunder, or any general or special law to the
contrary, the Board of Selectmen for the Town of Marblehead approves the application
for outdoor seating submitted by Donna Oliviero for Caffe Italia, located at 10 School
Street in Marblehead, Massachusetts, provided that the operation remains in substantial
compliance with the documents, plans and statements contained within said application
and submitted therewith, which documents are incorporated herein by reference. This
approval is freely revocable upon the determination of the Health Department, Chief of
Police, Board of Health, or their designees that the operation of the outdoor dining area is
negatively impacting public health and safety. Further, this approval shall automatically
be rescinded on November 1, 2020 or upon the recission of Executive Order COVID-19,
No. 35, whichever shall come first and is subject to final approval by the Town
Administrator, Police and Fire. Moved by Mrs. Jacobi, seconded by Mr. Nye. On a
polled vote the Board voted as follows: Mr. Nye, in favor; Mrs. Jacobi, in favor; Mr.
Grader, in favor; Mr. Christensen, in favor; Mrs. Belf-Becker, in favor.

CHAMBER OF COMMERCE. Celebrate Marblehead. Beth Ferris, Executive
Director, appeared before the Board seeking approval for the 7th year of “Celebrate
Marblehead”, a program that supports and offers opportunities to local businesses.

Motion made and seconded to approve the request from Beth Ferris, Chamber of
Commerce, to hold “Celebrate Marblehead” June 11 – October 31, 2020 and to allow the
businesses in Town the following considerations subject to receipt of the required
Certificate of Insurance and compliance with ADA, Fire, Building, Zoning and Health
Department requirements. and in accordance with all State Guidelines and COVD-19
Safety Protocols:
         Allow tables and/or displays outside of stores
            o Business will remain compliant with all ADA, fire, building and zoning
                 codes.
            o Tables and/or displays will allow for the required 36 inches of sidewalk
                 space, excluding curb, for handicapped accessibility requirements.
            o Sidewalk space will allow for 48 inches, however, an unobstructed 36-
                 inch path of travel, excluding curb, will be maintained past any sidewalk
                 obstruction. Such obstructions include but not limited to utility poles, mail
                 boxes, trees and open areas around them, street lights, traffic signal bases
                 and pre-cast foundations and other signal hardware, hydrants, signs and
                 poles.
            o It is preferred that tables and chairs are made of natural materials, ie: wood
                 or metal.
         Permit outdoor entertainment from Thursday–Sunday from 10 AM–8
            PM
            o Business will remain compliant with all ADA, fire, building and zoning
                 codes.
            o Entertainment will only include small or solo performances without
                 amplification.
         Restaurant outdoor seating
                 A separate application must be submitted to the Board of Selectmen for
                 outdoor restaurant seating.
         Allow small beautification projects
            o Business will remain compliant with all ADA, fire, building and zoning
                 codes:
            i.e. flower pots along sidewalks- placement will allow for the required 36
            inches of sidewalk space for handicapped accessibility requirements.
Moved by Mrs. Jacobi, seconded by Mr. Nye. On a polled vote the Board voted as
follows: Mr. Nye, in favor; Mrs. Jacobi, in favor; Mr. Grader, in favor; Mr. Christensen,
in favor; Mrs. Belf-Becker, in favor
