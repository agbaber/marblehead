Financial Update and Essex Tech Enrollment (00:28:00–00:38:00)

a. Superintendent John Robidoux provided update on Essex Tech enrollment and lottery system.
    • Essex Tech serves 17 communities; now uses lottery system due to regulations prohibiting consideration of
    grades, recommendations, or disciplinary records
    • Essex Tech proposed minimum 10-seat allocation for Marblehead based on three-year average; larger
    districts proposed percentage-based allocation increasing Marblehead's minimum from 10 to 33 students
b. Financial implications discussed.
    • Current Marblehead applicants: 33 (class of 2028), 29 (class of 2029), 28 (class of 2030)
    • Chapter 70 funding follows students to Essex Tech; potential cost could reach half a million dollars
    depending on allocation method
    • Superintendent preferred three-year average method; planned continued discussions with Essex Tech
    leadership

User Fees and Tuition Proposal (01:03:00–01:16:00)

a. Superintendent John Robidoux presented 2026-27 user fees and tuition with 3% increase.

    • HS athletic fees: 1st season $555, 2nd $505, 3rd $445; MS: 1st $270, 2nd $215, 3rd $165
    • Non-athletics: HS clubs $300, elementary activities $300; annual max increased to $2,125
    • Pre-K half day $5,150, full day $7,210; full-day kindergarten $4,420; transportation (K-6 <2 mi) $260
b. Fee waivers available for families meeting USDA income eligibility or directly certified through virtual
gateway; no students turned away for inability to pay.
c. Committee member Melissa Clucas raised concern that fees are high compared to surrounding towns and
charging for kindergarten is a moral issue.
d. A motion was made by Henry Gwazda and seconded by Melissa Clucas to approve the 2026-27 user fees and
tuition as outlined.
    • The motion passed 3–1, with votes in favor: Henry Gwazda, Kate Schmeckpeper, Jennifer Schaeffner; and
    vote against: Melissa Clucas.
e. A motion was made by Melissa Clucas and seconded by Henry Gwazda to place the School Department Capital
Needs article on the warrant.
    • The motion passed 4–0, with votes in favor: Kate Schmeckpeper, Henry Gwazda, Jennifer Schaeffner,
    Melissa Clucas.
