                                        Minutes
    Marblehead School Committee Policy Subcommittee Meeting
                          8/28/25

Members present: Jenn Schaeffner, Kate Schmeckpeper

Absent: none

Policy Review and Updates for 2025-2026 (00:00:00–00:51:00)

Call to order: 11:04AM

a. The subcommittee reviewed comprehensive MASC policy updates from March 2023
through 2024-25, categorizing them into minor edits, substantive changes, and new policies
requiring adoption
   • Some policies approved in June may not have been uploaded to the website
b. Specific policies requiring immediate attention with December 31st deadlines were
identified:
   • EFE Civil Rights Complaint Policy for Child Nutrition Programs needed adoption
   • IKF Competency Determination and graduation requirements policies required
   Interim Superintendent review before deadline
   • ILD Student Submission to Educational Surveys discussed for potential September 4th
   adoption
c. The committee established a systematic processing approach:
   • Minor edits would be redlined and presented as batches with adequate review time
   • Substantive changes would be sent to Interim Superintendent by letter categories (A-B,
   C-D-E, etc.)
   • New policies would be handled separately with operational feasibility review
d. Operational concerns were raised regarding policy implementation:
   • Questions about current district raffle practices related to gifts and solicitation policies
   • Concerns about door-to-door solicitation language conflicting with current fundraising
   practices
   • Discussion of survey consent requirements and student handbook operationalization
e. Future meetings were scheduled: September 30th at 8:30 AM, November 20th at 11:00
AM, December 18th at 11:00 AM, January 22nd at 11:00 AM, February 26th at 11:00 AM,
and April 30th at 11:00 AM
   • September 30th meeting would focus on A and B category policy reviews
f. Technical improvements discussed included creating hyperlinked indexes and ensuring
proper alphabetization to improve policy manual usability for staff and community
members

2nd: Jenn Schaeffner
