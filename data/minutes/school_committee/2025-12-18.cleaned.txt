Budget Items and Copier Contract Discussion (01:33:00–01:47:00)

a. Copier contract overview
    • Current contract expires January 2026; district has 29 machines total; current annual cost approximately
    $129,000
b. Vendor evaluation and recommendation
    • Three vendors submitted proposals: Ricoh, Konica Minolta, and Canon
    • Konica Minolta recommended: comparable cost to Ricoh, faster machines, true cost-per-copy billing
    • Estimated savings of $22,000/year ($88,000 over 48-month term); includes Papercut print management
    software
c. Lease terms
    • 48-month lease selected; 60 months deemed too long for school environment; requires select board approval
    under Mass General Law
    • District locked into machines for lease term but can adjust at renewal using Papercut volume data
d. Motion and vote
    • A motion was made by Henry Gwazda and seconded by Melissa Clucas to approve the vendor selection and
    contract
    • The motion passed 4–0, with votes in favor: Jennifer Schaeffner, Al Williams, Henry Gwazda, Melissa
    Clucas

Enrollment Update and Trends Analysis (01:47:00-02:05:00)

a. Enrollment data and trends
    • Julia Ferreira and Superintendent John Robidoux presented enrollment analysis showing negative 3.8%
    annual average change from 2019 to present
    • Declining enrollment is statewide due to low birth rates; no grade level currently exceeds 200 students
    • Graduation-to-entry gap exists annually with larger classes graduating than entering

b. Student transfers and alternative schools
    • Last year: 56 students moved out of state, 6 enrolled in homeschool
    • Resident students attending alternative schools: Epstein Hillel Academy (32), Marblehead Charter Schools
    (82), Pingree (41), St. John's Prep (78), Essex Tech (35)
    • Approximately 81-83% of resident students attend public schools, consistent with similar districts
    • Committee member requested distinguishing between students who moved versus those remaining as
    residents but attending private school
c. Regional factors and next steps
    • Pioneer Institute identified Marblehead as having second-lowest birth rate in state; limited new housing and
    aging population contribute to decline
    • District reduced approximately 1,000 students over ten years; staffing adjustments complicated by high-needs
    student distribution
    • Administration examining transition years for retention strategies and conducting class-by-class enrollment
    reviews for budgeting

Step Three Grievance Hearing Regarding Lead Teacher Stipend (02:35:00–03:46:00)

a. Grievance presentation by Brigitte Karns, English teacher at Marblehead Veterans Middle School
    • Served as sole lead English teacher since 2008 (16 years); position split June 2024, approximately one month
    after she spoke publicly about antisemitism in Marblehead Public Schools
    • Alleges discrimination based on being Jewish and a Zionist, and retaliation for speaking out; split affects
    current salary and retirement calculations ($3,500–$4,000 annual stipend)

b. Historical context and legal representation
    • Ms. Karns stated English position was only one split in 2024; CBA and job posting indicate five lead teacher
    positions at Veterans Middle School, one per subject
    • Attorney Yael Magen argued timing suggests retaliation; stated MCAD complaint would be withdrawn if
    grievance resolved favorably
c. Superintendent John Robidoux's response
    • Noted grievance filed with new principal a year after split; found nothing compelling to indicate antisemitism
    motivated decision; stated splitting stipends is past practice district-wide
d. Committee discussion
    • Ms. Karns confirmed no union representation during grievance process; stated she does not feel safe at work
    and has experienced social isolation since speaking out
e. Motion to enter executive session
    • A motion was made by Henry Gwazda and seconded by Melissa Clucas to enter executive session for
    multiple purposes including deliberation on the step three grievance, collective bargaining strategy, potential
    litigation, and compliance with open meeting law requirements
    • The motion passed 3–1, with votes in favor: Al Williams, Henry Gwazda, Melissa Clucas; vote against:
    Jennifer Schaeffner
    • The committee entered executive session without intent to return to open session at 9:48pm
