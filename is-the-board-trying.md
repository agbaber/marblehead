<div class="takeaway takeaway--neutral">
  <strong>Short answer: yes, with qualifications.</strong> From FY19 through FY26, the Select Board and School Committee engaged with structural fiscal issues in about 330 distinct moments, including 84 formal votes and 25 warrant article actions. Engagement was mostly <em>reactive</em> to named pressures (special-education cost spikes, bargaining windows, state grant-eligibility changes) rather than <em>proactive</em> policy design. Revenue-side reform is thin (23 attempts, mostly small fee tweaks or one-off home rule petitions). Healthcare and pensions, both named in meetings as major cost drivers, show only three discrete policy attempts combined. Finance Committee minutes were requested but are not yet available, so FinCom deliberations do not appear directly.
</div>

### Key findings at a glance

- **Substantive engagement is the norm, not the exception.** In the 174 meetings where fiscal topics were on the agenda, 331 distinct attempts were identified.
- **Reactive over proactive.** Most attempts respond to an immediate pressure (a shortfall, a contract deadline, a ballot window). Few initiate new policy.
- **Capital and structural deficit dominate.** Capital facilities (71 entries) and structural deficit and reserves (88) together account for nearly half of the record.
- **Revenue-side reform is limited.** 23 revenue-diversification entries, concentrated in small fees, a senior tax exemption home rule petition, and cannabis host agreements.
- **Healthcare and pension policy is near-absent in minutes.** 0 healthcare entries and 3 pension or <abbr class="g" title="Other Post-Employment Benefits">OPEB</abbr> entries across seven fiscal years. The cost drivers are named but discrete policy moments are not.
- **Finance Committee is a coverage gap.** A public records request for FinCom minutes is pending with the Town Clerk's office; when received, the record will be updated.

Three brief sections follow: how engagement shifted across eras, how the two bodies differ, and what the data does not show. The companion page, <a href="what-have-we-tried.html">What have we tried?</a>, lists the attempts grouped by policy area.

## How the record shifts across eras

Early years (FY19 to FY20) centered on the new Gerry School project. Two landmark votes stand out: the <abbr class="g" title="Massachusetts School Building Authority">MSBA</abbr> scope agreement <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/2019-03-18-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, March 18, 2019 minutes"></sup> and the debt exclusion that funded construction <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/2019-05-13-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, May 13, 2019 minutes"></sup>. Structural-deficit framing was largely absent.

Middle years (FY21 to FY23) brought the first explicit Town Administrator warning that free-cash reliance was "no longer sustainable" <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/2021-02-12-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, February 12, 2021 minutes"></sup>, and the FY24 override cycle that ended with a failed operating override at the June 2023 ballot. Both boards made substantive attempts: the School Committee approved an override-contingent level-services budget of $45.97M <sup class="cite" data-href="https://resources.finalsite.net/images/v1741690049/marbleheadschoolsorg/e0plkbxqbkz6ss7w3udr/2023327-Budget_Vote_Minutes-voted_462023.pdf" data-source="School Committee, March 27, 2023 minutes"></sup>, and after the override failed, voted to fund a comprehensive special education audit <sup class="cite" data-href="https://resources.finalsite.net/images/v1741690217/marbleheadschoolsorg/avrdpjkt2mqgqh8dv0zh/2023762023-Minutes-Voted_1052023.pdf" data-source="School Committee, July 6, 2023 minutes"></sup>.

Recent years (FY24 to present) show the highest volume and the most explicit structural framing. The Select Board formally prioritized long-term financial planning <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/2024-3-29-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, March 29, 2024 minutes"></sup>, engaged an outside override and restructuring specialist <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/2024-12-11-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, December 11, 2024 minutes"></sup>, and began preparing dual balanced-and-override FY27 budgets after projecting a $7M to $15M multi-year deficit <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/2025-12-10-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, December 10, 2025 minutes"></sup>.

## How the two bodies differ

Select Board entries concentrate on town-wide revenue mechanisms (senior tax exemption, cannabis host agreement, meals-tax discussion), multi-town shared services (ambulance, <abbr class="g" title="Department of Public Works">DPW</abbr>/Water-Sewer consolidation), and structural governance actions (Charter Committee, Finance Department restructuring).

School Committee entries concentrate on the school operating budget, special education out-of-district costs, collective bargaining, and school-facility capital projects. Override-contingent budgets and special education actions appear almost exclusively on the School Committee side.

Joint work is visible at specific moments: the September 2019 <abbr class="g" title="Memorandum of Agreement">MOA</abbr> <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/2019-09-25-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, September 25, 2019 minutes"></sup>, the December 2025 joint Select Board and Finance Committee session <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/2025-12-10-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, December 10, 2025 minutes"></sup>, and the proposed Eveleth Park and Recreation collaboration <sup class="cite" data-href="https://resources.finalsite.net/images/v1773772039/marbleheadschoolsorg/mnbn4dk4tvqwsj3bhuzb/SCminutes-2_13_26FacilitiesSubcommittee1.pdf" data-source="School Committee, February 13, 2026 minutes"></sup>.

## What the data does NOT show

The record is drawn from Select Board and School Committee minutes only. Finance Committee minutes were requested from the Town Clerk and are pending; FinCom deliberations appear here only as referenced in other bodies' meetings.

Healthcare and <abbr class="g" title="Group Insurance Commission">GIC</abbr> policy has zero entries in seven fiscal years. Health insurance is repeatedly named as a cost driver, for example in the December 2025 reference to "double-digit increases in healthcare and pensions" <sup class="cite" data-href="https://marbleheadma.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/2025-12-10-Select-Board-Minutes.pdf" data-source="Select Board, December 10, 2025 minutes"></sup>, but the minutes reviewed record no discrete <abbr class="g" title="Group Insurance Commission">GIC</abbr>-specific policy attempt, plan-design review, or contribution-rate decision. Pension and <abbr class="g" title="Other Post-Employment Benefits">OPEB</abbr> policy has three entries total. Actuarial updates and Retirement Board proceedings happen in other venues.

Minutes themselves vary in completeness. Some entries reflect votes without recorded deliberation; others reflect extended discussion without a vote.
