Revenue vs Expenses Under Each Override Tier

Forecast of total revenue and expenses FY27–FY30 under the phased override rollout proposed by Town Administrator Thatcher Kezer. Expenses grow at ~5.4%/yr; baseline revenue grows at ~2.5%/yr; override amounts follow the three-year phase-in schedule and then grow with the levy. The line is expenses; each bar is one revenue scenario.

Under Kezer's phased rollout schedule (Year 1: 14–29% of tier max, Year 2: ~70%, Year 3: 100%), no tier closes the annual gap in any single year. Tier 3 comes closest in FY29, when the full $15M is active and revenue runs just $0.5M short of expenses. In FY27 and FY28 every scenario leaves a meaningful shortfall; in FY30 even Tier 3 runs ~$4M short, and the shortfall widens every year after because expense growth (5.4%/yr) continues to outpace revenue growth (2.5%/yr). Any unfilled shortfall has to be closed through cuts, free cash, or other one-time sources.

FY27 figures are from the Town Administrator's State of the Town presentation (January 2026): expenses $109.5M, recurring revenue $101M, gap $8.47M. See SOURCE_LOOKUP.

FY28–FY30 projected using: expenses at 5.4%/yr (Finance Committee blended rate: healthcare 9%, pensions 8.5%, salaries 5%, other 3%, weighted by FY26 budget share); baseline revenue at 2.5%/yr (Proposition 2.5 levy growth, which dominates the revenue base).

Override amounts follow the three-year phase-in schedule documented on How the override works: Tier 1 draws $1.3M (FY27), $6.3M (FY28), $9.0M (FY29+); Tier 2 draws $2.8M, $8.7M, $12.0M; Tier 3 draws $4.3M, $10.5M, $15.0M. From FY30 onward the override amount grows with the levy at 2.5%/yr. Under Proposition 2.5 the town could legally levy each tier's full amount starting FY27; the phased schedule is Kezer's proposal, not a legal constraint.

These projections become less reliable further out. Actual results will depend on GIC rate decisions, collective bargaining outcomes, state aid, property growth, and other factors not modeled here.
