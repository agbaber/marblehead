Marblehead vs. Swampscott: Residential Tax Rate

Rate per $1,000 of assessed value, FY2003 - FY2026. Source: MA Department of Revenue.

Marblehead (single rate)
Swampscott (residential rate, split)
Swampscott's residential rate has been higher than Marblehead's in every year since FY2003, even though Swampscott uses a split rate that shifts tax burden onto commercial properties (Marblehead uses a single rate for everyone). In FY2026, Swampscott's residential rate ($12.00) is 40% higher than Marblehead's ($8.56). Both rates move inversely with property values, but the gap between the two towns has persisted for over two decades.

Source: Massachusetts Department of Revenue, Division of Local Services, Tax Rates by Class FY2003-FY2026.

Swampscott uses a split tax rate: residential ($12.00) and commercial/industrial ($22.15) in FY2026. Marblehead uses a single rate ($8.56) for all property classes.

Tax rates move inversely with property values. When values rise, rates fall (and vice versa) because the same levy is spread across a larger or smaller base.
