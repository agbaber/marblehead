Where Does Marblehead's Override Land?

Every operating override of $2M or more since FY2020, plotted by amount and fiscal year. Marblehead's proposed FY2027 override ($8.47M) marked for context.

  • Operating overrides only. Debt exclusions (capital projects, school buildings) not included
  • 65 override elections of $2M+ since FY2020. 28 passed, 34 failed, 3 split
  • At the $5M-10M range where Marblehead's ask sits, 8 of 15 passed (53%)
  • Marblehead's prior attempts ($2.5M in FY2024, $3.1M in FY2023) both failed at smaller amounts
  • Comparable recent wins: Braintree $8M, Belmont $8.4M, Milton $8.8M. Comparable losses: Bridgewater $8M, Malden $8.2M

Notes and sources

Override vote data from the Massachusetts Department of Revenue, Division of Local Services Municipal Databank.

Operating overrides only; debt exclusions not included. Where a town placed multiple override questions on the same ballot, all amounts are combined into a single per-election total. If a majority of questions passed, the election is coded as a win; if a majority failed, a loss; if evenly split, shown as "split" and excluded from pass rate calculations.

Marblehead's proposed FY2027 override ($8.47M across three ballot tiers) has not yet been voted on. It is shown as a dashed circle for reference only.

Stoneham FY2027 ($21.8M combined across two votes, one passed at $9.3M) is off the chart's x-axis and noted in the tooltip data but not plotted.

FY2027 data is partial as of April 2026.
