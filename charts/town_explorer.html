Town Explorer: All 351 Massachusetts Communities

Population, income, property wealth, and tax burden for every MA municipality. Source: MA DOR DLS FY2026/FY2027.

Instead of a curated peer set, this page shows all 351 municipalities. Use the filters to define your own comparison group. Marblehead appears in its sorted position (highlighted) and as a pinned reference row at the top. Default filters exclude very small towns (population under 5,000), very large cities (over 100,000), and island/resort communities with extreme property values.

Sources and methodology

Sources. Population, DOR Income, and EQV: MA DOR DLS, DOR Income and EQV Per Capita FY2027 (pulled April 15, 2026). Average single-family tax bill: DOR DLS FY2026. Bill as % of income: average SF tax bill divided by DOR per-capita income.

Data files. Raw CSVs: income, EQV, and population, tax bill as % of income.

Default filters. Population 5,000 to 100,000 excludes hamlets too small for meaningful comparison and large cities with fundamentally different fiscal structures. EQV/capita capped at $1,000,000 excludes island and resort communities (Nantucket, Chilmark, Edgartown, Provincetown, etc.) where seasonal property inflates per-capita valuations beyond relevance for year-round suburban comparison.

"Similar to Marblehead" preset. Population 10,000 to 35,000, DOR income per capita $60,000 to $175,000, EQV per capita $250,000 to $700,000. These thresholds are a convenience starting point. Adjust them to explore different peer definitions.

What is EQV? Equalized Valuation is the DOR's estimate of the full market value of all taxable property in a municipality, adjusted to a uniform standard across the state. It allows apples-to-apples comparison of property wealth between towns that assess at different ratios or on different revaluation cycles.

What is DOR Income? DOR Income is the total personal income of residents as estimated by the DOR for Cherry Sheet aid distribution. It is not the same as Census median household income or per-capita personal income from the Bureau of Economic Analysis. It is the standard income metric used in Massachusetts municipal finance formulas.
