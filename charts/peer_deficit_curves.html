Peers vs. the 2½ Baseline

How far each town's general fund revenue and expenditure has grown above (or below) a FY2002 base compounding at 2.5% per year, the Proposition 2½ levy growth cap.

Every line starts at 0% in FY2002. A flat line means the town's revenue or spending grew at exactly 2.5% per year. A line that climbs means growth outpaced 2.5%; that can come from successful override votes (new levy authority), from new state aid or local receipts, or from economic growth revaluing the tax base. A line that drops means growth was slower than 2.5%.

Notes and sources

Baseline choice. 2.5% is the Proposition 2½ levy growth cap. It applies strictly to a town's property tax levy, not to total general fund revenues or expenditures. Using it as a common yardstick means a flat line represents the town growing at the statutory floor, and climb or drop shows how far actual general fund totals have moved above or below that floor. It is a comparison tool, not a budgeting ratio.

Data gaps. Marblehead's FY2025 Schedule A has not yet been filed with DOR, so the FY2025 point for Marblehead is omitted from both panels. Every other peer has FY2025 data as of today. FY2020 shows a visible step in several towns because GASB 84 moved fiduciary funds into governmental funds that year, which inflates reported general fund revenues and expenditures without changing actual activity.

Source. Marblehead Division of Local Services, Schedule A General Fund, FY2002 through FY2025. Override ballot questions from the DOR DLS Municipal Databank, filtered to the 15 towns shown.

Reading the readings. Growth rates are compound annual growth rates between FY2002 and the last year with data. "Above baseline" uses 2.5% per year from FY2002 as the reference. Override counts are Proposition 2½ operating override ballot questions that passed in this window; debt exclusions and capital exclusions are not counted.
