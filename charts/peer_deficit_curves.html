Notes and sources

Scale. Each panel uses its own y-axis range (town totals differ by an order of magnitude). Read slope and crossover within a panel; do not compare y-values across panels.

Data. General fund revenues and expenditures from the Massachusetts Division of Local Services (DLS) Schedule A General Fund report. The FY report year is the fiscal year ending June 30. DLS general fund totals follow the Uniform Massachusetts Accounting System (UMAS) and do not reconcile line-for-line with ACFR governmental-fund totals (different fund groupings).

Override votes. Proposition 2½ operating override ballot questions from the DOR DLS Municipal Databank, filtered to the 15 towns shown. Wins and losses are both marked; not every year a town voted is an override year.

Volatility caveats. The FY2020 GASB 84 implementation moved fiduciary funds into governmental funds for several towns, producing a one-year step in reported revenues or expenditures that is accounting, not activity. Duxbury, Cohasset, and Hingham are small enough that one-time capital projects or grant drawdowns can produce single-year spikes; look at three-year moving trend rather than year-over-year when interpreting.

Editorial framing. Crossover points (where expenditures exceed revenues) are structural signals, not judgments. A town can run a planned drawdown from free cash in a given year without it indicating a long-term problem; conversely, revenue exceeding expenditure is how reserves build up in non-deficit years.
