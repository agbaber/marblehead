The Override Ratchet Across Peer Towns

Permanent levy authority added through passed Proposition 2½ operating overrides, FY2002 to FY2025. Measured as a percent of each town's FY2002 tax levy.

Every passed operating override permanently raises a town's levy ceiling by the overridden amount, and that amount then compounds at 2.5% per year for every year afterward. This chart tracks each town's cumulative override-added levy authority, rebased to its own FY2002 property tax levy so towns of different sizes are directly comparable. A flat line at zero means the town has not passed an operating override since FY2002. A line that steps up shows both when wins happened and how much permanent levy authority each win added.

Notes, methodology, sources

What this measures. For each passed operating override, the chart tracks the dollar amount voted onto the permanent levy base, then compounds that amount at 2.5% per year forward through FY2025 (the Proposition 2½ growth cap). The running total of these amounts is divided by the town's FY2002 tax levy from DLS Schedule A to produce a percent of base. This isolates how much additional permanent taxing authority each town has obtained through the override ballot process, holding other revenue sources aside.

What this does not measure. Actual tax levy, which depends on new-growth additions, unused levy capacity decisions, and debt-exclusion overrides that are not part of the permanent base. Operating expenditures or service levels. Whether a town needed the override or used the money well. Those are separate questions outside this chart.

Per-town base. FY2002 tax levy values used as the normalizer come from the "Taxes" column of the DLS Schedule A Revenue view for each town. For a frame of reference, Marblehead's FY2002 tax levy was $36.2 million; Needham's was $54.0 million; Lexington's was $74.0 million.

Overrides counted. Only ballot questions of vote-type "Override" that passed ("WIN") with fiscal year between FY2002 and FY2025 inclusive. Debt exclusions, capital exclusions, and stabilization transfers are not counted; only overrides that add permanent base authority. Ballot questions that failed are not counted. A town with several small overrides at the same ballot (Marblehead FY2005, Lexington FY2005, Needham FY1997) is the sum of those individual wins.

Source. Massachusetts Division of Local Services, Proposition 2½ Override and Debt Exclusion databank, accessed FY2026. FY2002 tax levy values from MA DLS Schedule A General Fund.
