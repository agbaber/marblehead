What Marblehead lost to keep the levy flat

Town-side municipal positions, FY18 to FY27. Each circle is one job.

Between FY18 and FY27, Marblehead's town government shrank from approximately 199 positions to approximately 190, according to Town Administrator Thatcher Kezer at the March 2026 budget hearings. The nine-position net loss is not evenly spread. The named losses concentrated in three departments: DPW, Police, and the Engineering Department (eliminated entirely and absorbed into DPW). Fire lost an unquantified number of positions through level-funding. Community Development & Planning is a separate case, addressed in the notes below.

The outlined circles below represent positions that were eliminated. Each row is one department; each circle is one full-time-equivalent (FTE) position.

Town total FY18 ~199 → FY27 ~190

Nine positions eliminated on the town side over nine years. Town population served (about 20,000) has not declined materially over the same period.

Each outlined circle represents one full-time-equivalent position that was eliminated, left unfilled long enough to be defunded, or absorbed into another department. "Lost" does not mean layoffs; most reductions came through attrition. The town total figures are approximations: Kezer cited "about 190" in public testimony rather than an audited headcount, and the FY18 DPW starting count is also an approximation (see notes).

About the school side. The school workforce also declined over the same period. DESE data shows Marblehead teacher FTE fell from 261.0 in FY18 to 224.8 in FY26, a drop of about 36 teachers. K-12 enrollment fell by a comparable percentage over the same window, so students-per-teacher was roughly unchanged. This chart does not show the school side because the staffing story there is different in kind: school positions declined roughly in proportion to enrollment, while the town workforce declined while the population served stayed essentially constant. For the school staffing and enrollment picture, see School enrollment vs. staffing over time.

On the DPW starting count. The FY18 DPW headcount is quoted as "30+ workers" by Select Board member Erin Noonan in late-2025 testimony. The chart uses ~31 as a plausible round figure; the exact FY18 audited headcount was not located in the primary sources available at publication. The ACFR functional category Public_Works was 36.51 FTE in FY18 declining to 31.0 FTE in FY24, consistent with but not identical to the testimony. If a precise department-line figure surfaces, the row will be updated.

On "all other departments." The ~134 (FY18) and ~139 (FY27) figures are derived as the residual: town total minus the named-department counts. FY18 residual = 199 minus (~31 DPW + 32 Police + 2 Engineering) = ~134. FY27 residual = 190 minus (19 DPW + 30 Police + 0 Engineering) = ~139. The implied net gain of about 5 in unnamed departments is consistent with the documented FY24 hire of an HR director, the FY26 creation of a four-person Community Development & Planning department, and the FY26 hire of a procurement director, partly offset by attrition elsewhere. Not an audited per-department figure.

About Community Development & Planning. This department is intentionally not drawn as its own row. It was created in FY26 with four staff (Director, Sustainability Coordinator, Grant Coordinator, Assistant Planner) and did not exist as a four-person department in FY18. The April 2026 override presentation states that two of those four positions were lost in FY26 itself (planner and sustainability), and the FY27 budget eliminates the Director and Grant Coordinator. The trajectory over the FY18-FY27 window is therefore 0 to 4 (FY26) to 1 (FY27), a net gain of one position relative to FY18. Showing it as "4 to 1" inside a "positions lost" chart would have misrepresented that history. The FY26-FY27 reduction is real and is disclosed here as a separate fact.
