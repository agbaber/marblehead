Tax Levy vs. Health Insurance Spending

Both indexed to FY2006 = 100. Actual values from audited financial reports and budget documents. Marblehead, FY2006-FY2027.

Both lines use actual data indexed to FY2006 = 100. The tax levy (green) grew 86% over 18 years. Health insurance spending (black) grew 110% over 21 years (through FY27 proposed). Health insurance is lumpier year to year because of claims experience, plan changes, and the GIC transition (FY2012), but the trend consistently outpaces revenue growth. FY20 health insurance data not available (dashed connector).

Tax levy from MA Department of Revenue (FY06-FY24). Health insurance from Annual Comprehensive Financial Report budget schedules (FY06-FY13) and Finance Committee reports (FY14-FY25). FY26-FY27 proposed budget (dashed).

Health insurance is the single largest line item the town does not directly control. Rates are set by the state Group Insurance Commission. The town's only lever is the employer/employee contribution split (currently 83%/17%), which requires agreement with the Public Employee Committee.
