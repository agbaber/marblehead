Rate and Bill: Four North Shore Towns

Two views of the same question. Marblehead, Swampscott, Melrose, and Stoneham, FY2003–FY2026. Source: MA Department of Revenue.

Tax rate and tax bill answer different questions. The rate is the price per $1,000 of assessed value, set by the town each year after the levy is certified by the state. The bill is what a homeowner actually pays, which depends on both the rate and the home's value. Two towns with the same tax rate can have very different average bills if their home values differ.

Marblehead has the lowest rate of the four towns and the second-highest average bill. The gap is explained by home values: Marblehead's median assessed home value ($1,010,100 in FY2026) is materially higher than Melrose ($853,264) or Stoneham.

Average single-family tax bill

Average single-family property tax bill for four North Shore towns, FY2006–FY2026. Marblehead's bill ($11,055) is the second-highest, behind Swampscott ($11,478), despite Marblehead having the lowest rate ($8.56) in the group. The difference is home values: Marblehead's median assessed value is $1,010,100 vs $853,264 in Melrose. The average bill reflects both the rate and the average home value, so towns with more expensive homes have higher bills even when their rates are lower.

Rate chart source: MA Department of Revenue, Division of Local Services, Tax Rates by Class FY2003–FY2026.

Bill chart source: MA Department of Revenue, Division of Local Services, "Average Single Family Tax Bill" report, FY2006–FY2026.

All four towns use split tax rates (residential vs commercial) except Marblehead, which uses a single rate for all property classes.

Melrose FY26 rate and bill reflect a $13.5M override passed November 2025. Stoneham's $9.3M override (Dec 2025) not yet reflected.

Marblehead override tiers on the rate chart are estimates: current rate + (override amount / $9.89B assessed value). Tier 1 ($9M) ~$9.46, Tier 2 ($12M) ~$9.76, Tier 3 ($15M) ~$10.06.

The rate and bill charts use different start years because the underlying DOR reports have different coverage.
