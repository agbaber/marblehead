Marblehead has had the lowest residential tax rate of all four towns for the entire 24-year period. Even with the maximum Tier 3 override ($15M), Marblehead's rate would rise to roughly $10.06, still below Melrose's post-override rate ($11.47), Swampscott ($12.00), and roughly equal to Stoneham ($10.06). The visible spike in Melrose's FY26 rate is the $13.5M override that passed after voters rejected $7.7M a year earlier and lived through 61 position cuts.

Source: MA Department of Revenue, Division of Local Services, Tax Rates by Class FY2003-FY2026.

All four towns use split tax rates (residential vs commercial) except Marblehead, which uses a single rate for all property classes.

Melrose FY26 rate includes $13.5M override passed November 2025. Stoneham's $9.3M override (Dec 2025) is not yet reflected in FY26 rate.

Marblehead "w/ Tier 3" is an estimate: current rate + ($15M / $9.89B assessed value).
